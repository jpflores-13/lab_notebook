2D ENS Differentiation Protocol

  • Show All Code
  • Hide All Code
  • View Source

Barber et al. 2019 · Majd et al. 2025

Published

June 14, 2026

NoteOverview

hPSC → vagal ENCC (Days 0–12) → crestospheres (Days 12–15) → 2D ENS culture (Days 15–40+). Always use Option B (E6, chemically defined, xeno-free) — required for downstream chromatin and genomics work. Full neurochemical maturity by Day 55–75. Cultures lasting >100 days achievable with careful feeding.

Configure experiment

Code 
viewof n_wells    = Inputs.number({label: "6-well plate wells", value: 6, min: 1, max: 96, step: 1})
viewof hpsc_line  = Inputs.select(["H9 (WA09)", "UCSF4", "WTC11", "Custom"], {label: "hPSC line"})
viewof pp121      = Inputs.toggle({label: "PP121 — NOS1⁺ enrichment (Days 15–20)", value: false})
Code 
CM2 = 9.5
total_cm2 = n_wells * CM2
md`**${n_wells} well(s) · ${total_cm2} cm² total · ${hpsc_line}**
All master mix volumes include +10% excess.`
Code 
pp121 ? md`::: {.callout-warning}
**PP121 active.** Add PP121 at 1–2 µM from Day 15 to Day 20 only. Enriches NOS1⁺ nitrergic neurons at the expense of CHAT⁺ cholinergic neurons. Remove completely from EN-C on Day 20 — extended treatment reduces viability.
:::` : md``

Media compositions

NoteMath audit — how every volume is calculated

Surface area — the foundation of all ENS volume calculations:

\[A_{total} = N_{wells} \times 9.5 \; cm^2/well\]

Cocktail volumes:

Phase Days µL/cm²
Cocktails A & B 0–4 200
Cocktail C early 6–8 400
Cocktail C late 10 600
EN-C feeds 15–40 200
EN-C late feeds 40+ 400

Factor dilutions — each growth factor is added as a small volume to E6 base. For Cocktail A, BMP4 at 1:10,000 dilution means: \(V_{BMP4} = V_{total} \times 10^{-4}\)

NC-C medium — scales by number of ULA wells (2 mL per well): \[V_{NC\text{-}C\;total} = N_{wells} \times 2 \; mL\]

Coating volumes (PLO, LM/FN) — 100 µL/cm²: \[V_{coating} = A_{total} \times 0.1 \; mL/cm^2\]

Code 
fmt_ens = (x, d = 1) => x.toFixed(d)

Cocktail A — Days 0–2 (200 µL/cm²)

NoteWhy these signals?

BMP4 (1 ng/mL) activates the NC gene regulatory network in the absence of serum. SB431542 blocks TGFβ to prevent mesendodermal fate. CHIR at 600 nM activates WNT to initiate NC specification. Lower CHIR here than in later cocktails — high WNT too early overspecifies posterior identity before NC identity is established.

Cocktail B — Days 2–4 (200 µL/cm²)

NoteWhy the CHIR increase?

CHIR rises from 600 nM to 1.5 µM at Day 2. Once NC identity is initiated, sustained higher WNT signaling drives proliferation and maintains SOX10+ progenitor state. BMP4 is dropped — it is only needed in the short induction window at Day 0.

Cocktail C — Days 6–12 (vagal patterning + retinoic acid)

WarningVolume increases — do not miss this
  • Days 6–8: 400 µL/cm² (doubles from Cocktail B)
  • Day 10: 600 µL/cm² (increases again)

This compensates for rising confluency. Insufficient volume at this stage causes monolayer detachment and lost cells.

WarningRetinoic acid handling

RA is light-sensitive and degrades rapidly at 37°C. Always make a fresh working stock from your –80°C aliquot on the day of use. Use amber tubes. Do not re-use opened aliquots.

NoteWhy RA starts at Day 6, not Day 0

Premature RA exposure (Days 0–4) actually inhibits NC induction by over-posteriorizing cells before NC identity is established. RA is only added once CHIR-driven NC specification is underway, then it caudalizes NC cells toward vagal/HOXB3+/HOXB5+ identity required for ENS competence.

Surface coating — PLO / Laminin / Fibronectin

WarningPrepare on Day 14 — not Day 15

The PLO overnight step means coating must start the day before plating. If you prepare it Day 15 morning you will lose hours of LM/FN incubation time and get poor cell attachment.

WarningNever let the coated surface dry

Aspirate the LM/FN solution and immediately add cells — no gap. A dried laminin surface is denatured and cells will not adhere.

Phase 1 — ENCC induction (Days –2 to 12)

Day –2: Replate hPSCs

ImportantDay 0 confluency is the #1 determinant of induction efficiency

Target: ~70% confluent monolayer on Day 0. Too sparse (<60%) or too dense (>85%) both significantly reduce CD49D+ yield at Day 12. If your cell line tends to grow slowly, plate at 5:4 instead of 5:6.

Step Action Detail
1 Aspirate E8-C; EDTA (500 µM in PBS) 3–5 min Longer than standard passaging — increases cell separation
2 Passage at 5:6 ratio (5 wells → 6 new wells) —
3 Plate on Matrigel or Vitronectin XF in E8-C Vitronectin preferred for fully defined system
4 Target: ~70% confluent monolayer on Day 0 Feed every other day with E8-C

Days 0–12: Medium change schedule

TipAdding medium without detaching cells

As the monolayer gets dense (especially Days 8–12), always tilt the plate and pipette slowly from the lower corner or side. Never pipette directly downward onto confluent cells — shear force detaches them.

Day 11–12 QC — do not proceed without passing

ImportantQC gate — required before Phase 2

If CD49D+ < 40%, check RA freshness, CHIR lot, and Day 0 confluency before troubleshooting further.

TipOptional: FACS purify at Day 12 for early progenitor experiments

Sort for CD49D+ using Pe/Cy7 anti-human CD49D (Biolegend 304314). Recommended if you need pure ENC progenitors for Day 11 assays. For most applications, the sphere phase (Days 12–15) provides sufficient enrichment without sorting.

Phase 2 — Crestosphere formation (Days 12–15)

WarningUltra-low attachment plates — must be certified ULA

Standard tissue culture plates will cause cells to adhere instead of forming spheres. Use Corning Costar certified ultra-low attachment plates only. This is the most common cause of Phase 2 failure.

Code 
Inputs.table([

  {Step: "2", Action: `Accutase: ${fmt_ens(total_cm2 * 0.1, 0)} µL (100 µL/cm²); 30 min 37°C`,                  Detail: "Full 30 min — do not shorten"},

], {layout: "auto"})
TipSphere size troubleshooting

Spheres should be 100–300 µm at Day 14, compact and round with smooth borders. If spheres have fused into large irregular bodies, gently pipette 2–3× with a P1000 to separate. Necrotic cores develop in large bodies and reduce Day 15 viability.

ImportantDay 15 QC

If CD49D+ is still <70% after the sphere phase, extend suspension culture to Day 16 before plating.

Phase 3 — 2D ENS differentiation (Days 15–40+)

WarningSerological pipette — not P1000 — for Day 15 dissociation

The narrower P1000 tip adds shear stress that causes excessive cell death. Use a 5 mL serological pipette for gentle dissociation into small clusters (5–20 cells). Single cell suspensions give very poor neuronal yields.

Code 
{
  const baseSteps = [
    {Step: "1", Action: "Prepare PLO/LM/FN coating on Day 14 (see Surface Coating section)",                       Detail: "Do not let surface dry"},
    {Step: "2", Action: "Swirl ULA plates; remove NC-C from circumference with P1000",                              Detail: "—"},
    {Step: "3", Action: "Wash with PBS; add Accutase (1 mL/well); 30 min 37°C",                                    Detail: "—"},
    {Step: "4", Action: "⚠ USE 5 mL SEROLOGICAL PIPETTE — 2–3 gentle rounds",                                      Detail: "Small clusters only; P1000 causes excess cell death"},
    {Step: "5", Action: "Spin 290×g 1 min; count viable cells with hemocytometer + trypan blue",                   Detail: "—"},
    {Step: "6", Action: `Plate 100,000 viable cells/cm² — ${fmt_ens(total_cm2 * 1e5 / 1e6, 1)}M cells total`,    Detail: "Move plates N/S/E/W after returning to incubator"}
  ]
  const pp121Step = {Step: "7", Action: "⚠ Add PP121 (1–2 µM) — NOS1⁺ enrichment", Detail: "Days 15–20 ONLY. Remove from EN-C Day 20. Depletes CHAT⁺ cholinergic neurons."}
  Inputs.table(pp121 ? [...baseSteps, pp121Step] : baseSteps, {layout: "auto"})
}
NoteWhy move plates N/S/E/W after plating?

Cells in suspension in EN-C distribute unevenly as soon as the plate is set flat. A gentle 4-direction rocking motion redistributes them before they settle, giving even coverage across the well. Do this within 2 minutes of placing in the incubator.

TipLong-term culture (Day 75+)

For cultures older than 50 days, wait until medium turns yellow before feeding — do not feed on a fixed schedule. Use FN/LM-supplemented EN-C at every feed. Cultures lasting >100 days are achievable. The main enemy of long-term cultures is aggressive medium changes that mechanically detach neurites.

QC checkpoints summary

Day Checkpoint Expected Observed Pass
0 hPSC confluency ~70% monolayer
6 SOX10 clustering GFP+ clusters visible
12 ENCC induction (FACS) CD49D+ ≥50%
12 Vagal identity (qPCR) HOXB3/5 ≥10-fold
15 Sphere enrichment (FACS) CD49D+ ≥80–90%
15 Sphere morphology 100–300 µm, compact, round
30 Early neurons (IF) TUBB3+/TrkC+ networks
40 Neuronal purity (FACS) >80% TUBB3+
40 Neurochemical (IF) NOS1, CHAT, 5HT, GABA
75 Full FACS CHAT~40%, NOS1~9%, 5HT~6%
NoteExpected neurochemical composition at Day 75

Per Barber et al. 2019 Fig. 4g and Fig. 5b: ~40% CHAT+ (cholinergic), ~9% GABA+, ~9% NOS1+ (nitrergic), ~6% 5HT+ (serotonergic), and ~25% SOX10+/GFAP+ (enteric glia). With PP121 treatment Days 15–20, NOS1+ increases 3–5× at the expense of CHAT+ (Majd et al. 2025).

Troubleshooting

TipCD49D+ <40% at Day 12

Most likely causes: wrong Day 0 confluency; degraded RA; CHIR lot variability. Check in this order: (1) confirm Day 0 was 70%; (2) make fresh RA from a new –80°C aliquot; (3) titrate your CHIR lot (1–5 µM) in a test well. CHIR activity varies significantly between lots.

TipSpheroids not forming (Phase 2)

Almost always a plate issue — verify Corning ULA certification. If using correct plates, check FGF2 activity (add BSA carrier) and reduce seeding density.

TipLow TUBB3+ at Day 40 (<60%)

Three most common causes: (1) single-cell dissociation at Day 15 — use serological pipette; (2) PLO/LM/FN surface dried before plating; (3) inactive GDNF lot. Test each in order.

TipSMA+ myofibroblast contamination

NC-derived SMA+ flat cells are the primary contaminant. They eventually cause neuronal detachment. Minimize by FACS-sorting CD49D+ at Day 12 if contamination is severe, or by reducing passaging ratio at Day –2.

### Cocktail C — Days 6–12 (vagal patterning + retinoic acid)

::: {.callout-warning}
## Volume increases — do not miss this
- Days 6–8: **400 µL/cm²** (doubles from Cocktail B)
- Day 10: **600 µL/cm²** (increases again)

This compensates for rising confluency. Insufficient volume at this stage causes monolayer detachment and lost cells.
:::

::: {.callout-warning}
## Retinoic acid handling
RA is light-sensitive and degrades rapidly at 37°C. Always make a fresh working stock from your –80°C aliquot on the day of use. Use amber tubes. Do not re-use opened aliquots.
:::

::: {.callout-note collapse="true"}
## Why RA starts at Day 6, not Day 0
Premature RA exposure (Days 0–4) actually inhibits NC induction by over-posteriorizing cells before NC identity is established. RA is only added once CHIR-driven NC specification is underway, then it caudalizes NC cells toward vagal/HOXB3+/HOXB5+ identity required for ENS competence.
:::

## Phase 1 — ENCC induction (Days –2 to 12)

### Day –2: Replate hPSCs

::: {.callout-important}
## Day 0 confluency is the #1 determinant of induction efficiency
Target: **~70% confluent monolayer** on Day 0. Too sparse (<60%) or too dense (>85%) both significantly reduce CD49D+ yield at Day 12. If your cell line tends to grow slowly, plate at 5:4 instead of 5:6.
:::

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Aspirate E8-C; EDTA (500 µM in PBS) **3–5 min** | Longer than standard passaging — increases cell separation |
| 2 | Passage at **5:6 ratio** (5 wells → 6 new wells) | — |
| 3 | Plate on Matrigel or Vitronectin XF in E8-C | Vitronectin preferred for fully defined system |
| 4 | Target: ~70% confluent monolayer on Day 0 | Feed every other day with E8-C |

### Days 0–12: Medium change schedule

::: {.callout-tip}
## Adding medium without detaching cells
As the monolayer gets dense (especially Days 8–12), always tilt the plate and pipette slowly from the lower corner or side. Never pipette directly downward onto confluent cells — shear force detaches them.
:::

## Phase 3 — 2D ENS differentiation (Days 15–40+)

::: {.callout-warning}
## Serological pipette — not P1000 — for Day 15 dissociation
The narrower P1000 tip adds shear stress that causes excessive cell death. Use a 5 mL serological pipette for gentle dissociation into small clusters (5–20 cells). Single cell suspensions give very poor neuronal yields.
:::

::: {.callout-note collapse="true"}
## Why move plates N/S/E/W after plating?
Cells in suspension in EN-C distribute unevenly as soon as the plate is set flat. A gentle 4-direction rocking motion redistributes them before they settle, giving even coverage across the well. Do this within 2 minutes of placing in the incubator.
:::

## Long-term culture (Day 75+)
For cultures older than 50 days, wait until medium turns yellow before feeding — do not feed on a fixed schedule. Use FN/LM-supplemented EN-C at every feed. Cultures lasting >100 days are achievable. ## Troubleshooting

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## CD49D+ <40% at Day 12
Most likely causes: wrong Day 0 confluency; degraded RA; CHIR lot variability. Check in this order: (1) confirm Day 0 was 70%; (2) make fresh RA from a new –80°C aliquot; (3) titrate your CHIR lot (1–5 µM) in a test well. CHIR activity varies significantly between lots.
:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## Spheroids not forming (Phase 2)
Almost always a plate issue — verify Corning ULA certification. If using correct plates, check FGF2 activity (add BSA carrier) and reduce seeding density.
:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## Low TUBB3+ at Day 40 (<60%)
Three most common causes: (1) single-cell dissociation at Day 15 — use serological pipette; (2) PLO/LM/FN surface dried before plating; (3) inactive GDNF lot. Test each in order.
:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## SMA+ myofibroblast contamination
NC-derived SMA+ flat cells are the primary contaminant. They eventually cause neuronal detachment. Minimize by FACS-sorting CD49D+ at Day 12 if contamination is severe, or by reducing passaging ratio at Day –2.
:::
