NoteOverview

In situ Hi-C captures genome-wide chromatin contacts by crosslinking, restriction digestion (MboI), proximity ligation, and sequencing. The protocol runs over 6 days. Key rate-limiting steps: restriction digest efficiency (Day 1 O/N), biotin fill-in (Day 2 — 5% excess only), and shearing target size (Day 4 300–500 bp).

ImportantBefore Day 0

Fill LN₂ thermos. Set two centrifuges to 4°C. Prepare 1% formaldehyde and 2M glycine stock. Enzymes stay in glycerol block on ice — do not pre-thaw MboI or Klenow.

Scaled master mixes

NoteMath audit — how every volume is calculated

Core formula (all standard mixes):

\[V_{total} = V_{per\_sample} \times N_{samples} \times 1.10\]

Formaldehyde crosslinking — working backwards from 1% final in ~10 mL/sample:

\[V_{16\%\;stock} = \frac{N_{cells} \times 1.1}{0.16} \; mL\]

Glycine quench — to reach 0.2M final from 2M stock:

\[V_{2M\;glycine} = \frac{N_{cells} \times 1.1}{10} \; mL\]

Biotin fill-in — ⚠️ 5% excess ONLY (not 10%):

\[V_{total} = V_{per\_sample} \times N_{samples} \times \mathbf{1.05}\]

Biotin-14-dATP costs ~$800/vial. The 5% exception is intentional and hardcoded.

Day 0 — Crosslinking

WarningFormaldehyde — fume hood only

All formaldehyde steps must be performed in a fume hood. Collect formaldehyde-exposed waste separately for disposal per institutional guidelines. Glycine quench must be added in the hood before moving tubes to bench.

TipCounting cells before crosslinking

Plate an extra well at the same density as your experimental wells and count from that well on Day 0. Do not count from your experimental wells — removing cells for counting changes the density of the remaining cells.

Code 
fa = (n_cells_M * 1.1 / 0.16).toFixed(2)
gl = (n_cells_M * 1.1 / 10).toFixed(2)

Day 1 — Lysis & MboI restriction digest (~2 hr + O/N)

WarningMboI handling

Keep MboI in the glycerol block on ice at all times. Return to –20°C immediately after dispensing. Never let it sit at room temperature — glycerol-containing enzymes lose activity quickly when warmed.

NoteWhy 0.5% SDS + Triton X-100 quench?

SDS permeabilizes nuclei to allow MboI access to chromatin. However, SDS also inhibits MboI, so it must be quenched with Triton X-100 before adding enzyme. The 62°C step ensures denaturation of surface proteins without denaturing chromatin structure.

Step Action Detail
1 Set microcentrifuge 4°C; thermomixer 62°C; oven 37°C —
2 Thaw crosslinked cells on ice; add 300 µL lysis buffer + PI Ice 15 min
3 Spin 2500×g 5 min 4°C; discard supernatant —
4 Wash pellet with 500 µL lysis buffer; spin again —
5 Resuspend in 50 µL 0.5% SDS; 62°C 7 min —
6 Add 145 µL ddH₂O + 25 µL 10% Triton X-100; quench SDS 37°C 15 min; avoid foaming
7 Add 25 µL 10× NEBuffer 2 + 20 µL MboI (100 U) Return MboI to –20°C immediately
8 Digest O/N (or ≥2 hr) at 37°C with rotation Hybridization oven
ImportantChecklist

Day 2 — Biotin fill-in, ligation & crosslink reversal (~6.5 hr + O/N)

Warning5% excess only for biotin fill-in — not 10%

Biotin-14-dATP is extremely expensive. The biotin fill-in master mix uses 5% excess (not the standard 10%). This is the only step in the protocol with a different excess factor.

NoteWhy proximity ligation at RT with slow rotation?

Proximity ligation must occur while chromatin is still condensed inside intact nuclei — this ensures only DNA ends that are spatially close get ligated. Elevated temperature or vigorous mixing would release chromatin and allow random intermolecular ligation, destroying the contact information.

Step Action Detail
1 Inactivate MboI: 62°C 20 min; cool to RT Set oven to 23°C (open door)
2 Add 50 µL biotin fill-in mix — 5% excess only 37°C 1.5h rotation
3 Add 900 µL ligation mix; RT 4h slow rotation Slowest oven speed; door open
4 Spin 2500×g 5 min; remove supernatant —
5 Resuspend in 546 µL crosslink reversal mix; 55°C 30 min Thermomixer
6 Continue 68°C O/N; no shaking —
ImportantChecklist

Day 3 — DNA precipitation (~3 hr + 6 hr at –80°C)

TipThis is a clean break point

DNA can sit dissolved in 1× Tris pH 8 at 4°C O/N after the precipitation step. If you need to pause, stop here — Day 4 shearing can be done the next morning.

Step Action Detail
1 Cool tubes to RT —
2 Add 874 µL 100% EtOH + 55 µL 3M NaAc pH 5.2 Invert to mix; –80°C 4–6h
3 Spin max speed 2°C 25 min; remove supernatant on ice Keep tubes cold throughout
4 Wash 800 µL 70% EtOH × 2; vacuum dry —
5 Dissolve in 130 µL 1× Tris pH 8; 37°C 1h Or 4°C O/N
ImportantChecklist

Day 4 — Shearing & size selection (~6.5 hr)

WarningTarget size is 300–500 bp — verify with gel before proceeding

If shearing is outside this range, the size selection will be off and library complexity will suffer. Run a 1:5 dilution on a 2% agarose gel before proceeding to AMPure. Do not skip the gel check.

TipAMPure size selection logic

The two-step AMPure protocol selects for 300–500 bp fragments. Step 1 (0.55×): large fragments (>500 bp) bind beads, small fragments (<500 bp) stay in supernatant — keep supernatant. Step 2 (add 0.15× more to supernatant): now 300–500 bp fragments bind — keep these beads. Fragments <300 bp remain in supernatant and are discarded.

Step Action Detail
1 Qubit BR; if >150 ng/µL dilute to 150 ng/µL in 130 µL —
2 Covaris LE220: Duty 25 / PIP 500 / 200 cyc/burst / 90 sec 130 µL microTUBE; fill level 6
3 Transfer + wash vial with 70 µL ddH₂O → 200 µL total —
4 Gel verify shearing: 1:5 dilution on 2% agarose Use bottom layer of gel
5 AMPure 0.55× (110 µL); keep supernatant <500 bp in supernatant
6 Add 0.15× more AMPure (30 µL) to supernatant; keep beads 300–500 bp on beads
7 Wash 700 µL 70% EtOH × 2; elute in 300 µL 1× Tris —
8 Qubit HS dsDNA; gel verify size selection Store –20°C
ImportantChecklist

Day 6 — PCR amplification & final cleanup (~2 hr)

TipCycle number

Target 7–8 cycles for 5M cells starting input. Use 4–6 cycles if starting input was higher (>10M cells). Never exceed 12 cycles — PCR duplicates inflate apparent contact counts and reduce unique pair complexity.

Code 
Inputs.table([
  {Step: "1", Action: `25 µL DNA+beads + 25 µL PCR mix; ${pcr_cycles} cycles`, Detail: "95°C 3 min → (98°C 20s/60°C 15s/72°C 30s) × N → 72°C 5 min"},
  {Step: "2", Action: "Add 200 µL 1× Tris → 250 µL; magnet; transfer supernatant", Detail: "Save beads at –20°C"},
  {Step: "3", Action: "AMPure 0.7× (175 µL); wash 700 µL 70% EtOH; resuspend 100 µL", Detail: "—"},
  {Step: "4", Action: "Second AMPure 0.7× (70 µL); wash × 2; elute 25 µL 1× Tris", Detail: "—"},
  {Step: "5", Action: "Qubit HS dsDNA; Tapestation", Detail: "Submit for sequencing"}
], {layout: "auto"})

## Day 0 — Crosslinking

::: {.callout-warning}
## Formaldehyde — fume hood only
All formaldehyde steps must be performed in a fume hood. Collect formaldehyde-exposed waste separately for disposal per institutional guidelines. Glycine quench must be added **in the hood** before moving tubes to bench.
:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## Counting cells before crosslinking
Plate an extra well at the same density as your experimental wells and count from that well on Day 0. Do not count from your experimental wells — removing cells for counting changes the density of the remaining cells.
:::

## Day 1 — Lysis & MboI restriction digest (~2 hr + O/N)

::: {.callout-warning}
## MboI handling
Keep MboI in the glycerol block on ice at all times. Return to –20°C immediately after dispensing. Never let it sit at room temperature — glycerol-containing enzymes lose activity quickly when warmed.
:::

::: {.callout-note collapse="true"}
## Why 0.5% SDS + Triton X-100 quench?
SDS permeabilizes nuclei to allow MboI access to chromatin. However, SDS also inhibits MboI, so it must be quenched with Triton X-100 before adding enzyme. The 62°C step ensures denaturation of surface proteins without denaturing chromatin structure.
:::

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Set microcentrifuge 4°C; thermomixer 62°C; oven 37°C | — |
| 2 | Thaw crosslinked cells on ice; add 300 µL lysis buffer + PI | Ice 15 min |
| 3 | Spin 2500×g 5 min 4°C; discard supernatant | — |
| 4 | Wash pellet with 500 µL lysis buffer; spin again | — |
| 5 | Resuspend in 50 µL 0.5% SDS; 62°C 7 min | — |
| 6 | Add 145 µL ddH₂O + 25 µL 10% Triton X-100; quench SDS | 37°C 15 min; avoid foaming |
| 7 | Add 25 µL 10× NEBuffer 2 + 20 µL MboI (100 U) | Return MboI to –20°C immediately |
| 8 | Digest O/N (or ≥2 hr) at 37°C with rotation | Hybridization oven |

::: {.callout-important}
## Checklist
- [ ] MboI returned to –20°C
- [ ] Samples rotating at 37°C O/N
:::

## Day 2 — Biotin fill-in, ligation & crosslink reversal (~6.5 hr + O/N)

::: {.callout-warning}
## 5% excess only for biotin fill-in — not 10%
Biotin-14-dATP is extremely expensive. The biotin fill-in master mix uses **5% excess** (not the standard 10%). This is the only step in the protocol with a different excess factor.
:::

::: {.callout-note collapse="true"}
## Why proximity ligation at RT with slow rotation?
Proximity ligation must occur while chromatin is still condensed inside intact nuclei — this ensures only DNA ends that are spatially close get ligated. Elevated temperature or vigorous mixing would release chromatin and allow random intermolecular ligation, destroying the contact information.
:::

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Inactivate MboI: 62°C 20 min; cool to RT | Set oven to 23°C (open door) |
| 2 | **Add 50 µL biotin fill-in mix — 5% excess only** | 37°C 1.5h rotation |
| 3 | Add 900 µL ligation mix; RT 4h slow rotation | Slowest oven speed; door open |
| 4 | Spin 2500×g 5 min; remove supernatant | — |
| 5 | Resuspend in 546 µL crosslink reversal mix; 55°C 30 min | Thermomixer |
| 6 | Continue 68°C O/N; no shaking | — |

## Day 4 — Shearing & size selection (~6.5 hr)

::: {.callout-warning}
## Target size is 300–500 bp — verify with gel before proceeding
If shearing is outside this range, the size selection will be off and library complexity will suffer. Run a 1:5 dilution on a 2% agarose gel before proceeding to AMPure. Do not skip the gel check.
:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
## AMPure size selection logic
The two-step AMPure protocol selects for 300–500 bp fragments. Step 1 (0.55×): large fragments (>500 bp) bind beads, small fragments (<500 bp) stay in supernatant — **keep supernatant**. Step 2 (add 0.15× more to supernatant): now 300–500 bp fragments bind — **keep these beads**. Fragments <300 bp remain in supernatant and are discarded.
:::

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Qubit BR; if >150 ng/µL dilute to 150 ng/µL in 130 µL | — |
| 2 | Covaris LE220: Duty 25 / PIP 500 / 200 cyc/burst / 90 sec | 130 µL microTUBE; fill level 6 |
| 3 | Transfer + wash vial with 70 µL ddH₂O → 200 µL total | — |
| 4 | Gel verify shearing: 1:5 dilution on 2% agarose | Use bottom layer of gel |
| 5 | AMPure 0.55× (110 µL); keep **supernatant** | <500 bp in supernatant |
| 6 | Add 0.15× more AMPure (30 µL) to supernatant; keep **beads** | 300–500 bp on beads |
| 7 | Wash 700 µL 70% EtOH × 2; elute in 300 µL 1× Tris | — |
| 8 | Qubit HS dsDNA; gel verify size selection | Store –20°C |

::: {.callout-important}

:::
